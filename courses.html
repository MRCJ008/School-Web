Courses
  • Science Biology
    The science biology course offers an in-depth study of life and living organisms, focusing on areas such as cell biology, genetics, evolution, human anatomy, plant physiology, and ecology. It combines theoretical knowledge with practical lab experiments, helping students understand biological processes at molecular and environmental levels. The course encourages critical thinking and analytical skills, preparing students for careers in medicine, biotechnology, research, and environmental sciences, while fostering a deeper appreciation of the complexity of life.

  • Science Computer
    The science computer course provides a comprehensive understanding of computer systems, programming, and software development. It covers key topics like algorithms, data structures, networking, databases, and cybersecurity. Students learn coding languages such as Python, Java, or C++, while engaging in practical lab work and real-world projects. The course enhances problem-solving, logical thinking, and technical skills, preparing students for careers in software engineering, IT, data science, and artificial intelligence, while equipping them for technological advancements in the digital world.

  • Science Electronics
    The science electronics course focuses on the study of electronic systems, circuits, and devices. It covers essential topics such as semiconductors, transistors, diodes, integrated circuits, digital electronics, and communication systems. Through hands-on lab experiments and theoretical lessons, students gain practical skills in designing and troubleshooting electronic circuits. This course develops analytical and problem-solving abilities, preparing students for careers in electronics engineering, telecommunications, automation, and related fields, fostering innovation in modern technology and electronic applications.

  • Commerce
    The commerce course provides a deep understanding of business, finance, economics, and accounting. Key subjects include business studies, economics, financial management, taxation, and marketing. Students learn practical skills in managing finances, analyzing markets, and understanding economic principles. The course develops critical thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making abilities, preparing students for careers in business, banking, entrepreneurship, and corporate management. It equips them with a strong foundation for pursuing advanced studies in commerce, economics, and related professional fields.
